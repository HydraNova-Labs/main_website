Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 14, 2026

1. Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how Atlas Bioindustries ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and handles information when you visit our website ("Site"). We are committed to being transparent about our data practices.

This Site is an informational website. We do not offer user accounts, membership portals, or e-commerce functionality. Our data collection is therefore limited in scope, as described below.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Automatically Collected Data

When you visit our Site, certain technical information is collected automatically through our hosting infrastructure and analytics services:

  • Internet Protocol (IP) address
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Referring URL and pages visited on this Site
  • Date and time of visit
  • Approximate geographic location (derived from IP address)

2.2 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our Site. Google Analytics collects data including pages viewed, time spent on the Site, and general usage patterns. Google Analytics uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to collect this information. The data is transmitted to and stored by Google on servers that may be located outside your country of residence.

Google's use of this data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. You may opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

2.3 Google Fonts

Our Site uses Google Fonts, a web font service provided by Google LLC. When you visit our Site, your browser makes a request to Google's servers to load these fonts. This request transmits your IP address and browser information to Google. We do not control this data transfer. Google's handling of this data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

2.4 Netlify Hosting Analytics

Our Site is hosted on Netlify. Netlify collects server-side analytics from web server logs, including IP addresses, page requests, and response data. This is processed at the infrastructure level and does not involve cookies or client-side tracking scripts. For more information, see Netlify's Privacy Policy.

2.5 Email Communications

Our Site provides an email address for visitors to contact us. If you send us an email, we will receive your email address and any personal information you include in your message. We use this information solely to respond to your inquiry and do not add you to any mailing list without your explicit consent.

3. How We Use Information

We use the information described above for the following purposes:

  • To monitor and maintain the security and performance of our Site
  • To understand how visitors use our Site and to improve its content
  • To respond to inquiries sent to us via email
  • To comply with applicable legal obligations

4. Cookies

Our Site itself does not set first-party cookies. However, Google Analytics sets cookies on your device to support its analytics functionality. These are third-party cookies governed by Google's policies.

You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some third-party services on this Site. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or delete existing cookies via their settings menu.

5. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share data in the following limited circumstances:

  • With Google LLC, as described above in connection with Google Analytics and Google Fonts
  • With Netlify Inc., as our hosting provider, which processes server log data
  • When required by law, regulation, or valid legal process
  • To protect the rights, property, or safety of Atlas Bioindustries, our users, or the public

6. Information for Visitors in the European Economic Area (EEA)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, this section applies to you in addition to the rest of this Privacy Policy.

6.1 Legal Basis for Processing

We rely on the following legal bases to process personal data under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f)): We process server log and analytics data to maintain the security of our Site and understand usage patterns. We have assessed that this processing is proportionate and does not override your privacy interests given the limited nature of the data collected.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): Where required, we rely on your consent for cookie-based tracking via Google Analytics. You may withdraw consent at any time by opting out via the Google Analytics Opt-out Add-on or by adjusting your browser's cookie settings.

6.2 International Data Transfers

Google Analytics and Google Fonts involve transferring data to Google LLC, which is based in the United States. Google relies on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission to safeguard such transfers. You can find more information at Google's Privacy Policy.

6.3 Your Rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights with respect to your personal data:

  • Right of Access: You may request a copy of any personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You may request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure: You may request deletion of your personal data, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You may request that we limit how we use your data.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object: You may object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at the address listed in Section 9. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law (generally 30 days).

7. Information for California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, this section applies to you under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA).

7.1 Categories of Personal Information Collected

In the preceding 12 months, we have collected the following categories of personal information:

  • Identifiers: IP addresses collected via server logs and Google Analytics
  • Internet or Network Activity: Browsing history on this Site, collected via Google Analytics
  • Geolocation Data: Approximate location derived from IP address

7.2 Sources and Purposes

This information is collected directly from your device when you visit our Site, and from third-party services (Google Analytics, Netlify). It is used for site security, analytics, and service improvement as described in Section 3.

7.3 Sale or Sharing of Personal Information

We do not sell personal information. We do not share personal information with third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising purposes, except as inherent in the use of Google Analytics (which may use data for its own purposes under its terms). You may opt out of this by using the Google Analytics Opt-out Add-on.

7.4 Your California Rights

California residents have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, or share
  • Request deletion of personal information we hold, subject to exceptions
  • Opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information
  • Non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights

To submit a request, please contact us using the information in Section 9. We will verify your identity and respond within 45 days as required by law.

8. Data Retention

We retain data for the following periods:

  • Server logs (Netlify): Retained per Netlify's standard infrastructure policies. Please refer to Netlify's Privacy Policy for specifics.
  • Google Analytics data: Retained for 26 months by default, per Google Analytics settings. This can be adjusted; if you have a data retention request, contact us.
  • Email correspondence: Retained as long as reasonably necessary to address your inquiry and for a reasonable period thereafter.
